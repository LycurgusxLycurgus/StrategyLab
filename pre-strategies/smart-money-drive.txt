* **Edge Hypothesis:** Gold intraday price action is driven by the Interbank Price Delivery Algorithm (IPDA). ## 2. White-Box Components (The PineScript Domain)
*This layer translates the Drive folders into explicit, human-readable rules to be coded in TradingView (PineScript).*

### Component 1: Market Context Filter (Folders 7 & 9 - KillZones & Fundamental)
*   **Time Filter:** Strategy is only active during specific KillZones:
    *   London KillZone: 02:00 - 05:00 EST
    *   New York KillZone: 07:00 - 10:00 EST
*   **Fundamental Filter:** No entries 30 minutes before or after high-impact USD news (NFP, CPI, FOMC).

### Component 2: State Machine & Structural Bias (Folders 1 & 2 - IPDA & Fractales)
*   **State Machine:** `IDLE` → `WATCHING_LIQUIDITY` → `POI_MITIGATED` → `CONFIRMATION` → `IN_TRADE`
*   **Structural Bias (Fractals):** The 15m trend is defined by the last two Swing Highs and Swing Lows (e.g., Higher Highs + Higher Lows = Bullish Bias).

### Component 3: Setup Detection (Folders 3, 4 & 5 - Liquidez, Orderblocks, FVG)
*   **Liquidity Sweep (Liquidez):** Price must pierce a recent Asian Session High/Low or a previous day's High/Low by a maximum of *X* ATR, then close back inside the range.
*   **Point of Interest (POI):** After the sweep, price must tap into a valid 15m **Orderblock** (last opposing candle before an impulsive move) or a **Fair Value Gap (FVG)** (3-candle imbalance).

### Component 4: Entry Logic (Folder 6 & 8 - Confirmaciones & Estrategia)
*   **Trigger (Change of Character - CHoCH):** Once the POI is tapped, we wait for a 15m candle to close past the most recent minor swing high/low in the opposite direction of the sweep.
*   **Execution:** Limit order placed at the newly formed FVG/Orderblock created by the CHoCH impulse.

### Component 5: Risk & Trade Management (Folders 10 & 11 - Backtesting & Trades)
*   **Stop Loss:** Placed 1 ATR below the sweep low (for longs) or above the sweep high (for shorts).
*   **Take Profit:** Fixed structural targets (next opposing liquidity pool) or a minimum of 2R (Reward-to-Risk).
*   **Management:** Move stop to Breakeven when price reaches 1R.

## 3. Black-Box Components (The Python/Render Domain)
*This layer acts as the ML Confirmation Filter. PineScript will detect the White-Box setup and send a Webhook to the Render service. The ML model will score the setup.*

### Component 1: Feature Engineering Pipeline (Context Variables)
When a setup is detected, the Python backend will calculate features that are hard to explicitly code in PineScript:
*   **Volatility Context:** Current 15m ATR vs. Daily ATR.
*   **Time Context:** Day of the week, exact hour/minute of the sweep.
*   **Cross-Asset Correlation:** Current 15m trend of DXY (US Dollar Index) and US10Y (Treasury Yields). *Rule: Gold longs have higher probability if DXY is bearish.*
*   **Sweep Magnitude:** How deep the liquidity grab was relative to average volume.

### Component 2: The Model (XGBoost / LightGBM)
*   **Target Variable:** Binary classification (1 = Trade hit 2R before Stop Loss; 0 = Trade hit Stop Loss first).
*   **Inference:** The model outputs a probability score (0.0 to 1.0).
*   **Threshold:** Only return a "TRADE_APPROVED" signal to TradingView if the model's confidence score is **> 0.65**.

## 4. System Architecture & Execution Plan
*How the two systems communicate to execute the Hybrid Strategy.*

1. **TradingView (PineScript):** Runs the White-Box logic. When `CONFIRMATION` state is reached, PineScript fires a JSON Webhook containing the entry price, stop loss, timestamp, and basic chart features.
2. **Render (Python/FastAPI):** Receives the JSON. Fetches auxiliary data (DXY, Yields) via free APIs (e.g., yfinance). Passes the feature vector to the pre-trained XGBoost model.
3. **Response:** Python returns a JSON: `{"action": "EXECUTE", "confidence": 0.72}`.
4. **Broker Execution:** If approved, TradingView (via a service like PineConnector or direct broker integration) executes the limit order.
